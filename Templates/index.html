About me

Always curious about life (just look at the picture!) and always wanting more from it.

Born and raised in Warsaw Poland, a pleasant path of life lied in front of me.

Young (on paper - I don't intend to get actually old) Krzysztof showed interest in a variety of fields. From chess, history, math, video games to playing and teaching tennis

I graduated from the top commercial university, completed a few internships - from small local business, through Ministry of Finance to a duty free retailer.

Finally I joined Nestle's graduate program where I first experienced multinational procurement best practices (and some corporate neocolonial approach to Eastern European branches).

Everything was going great (great team, promotion), but there was more to life, more to explore (that's right - Australia).

I changed companies so I could save up a bit more $ and use English more when the Working Holoiday Visa opportunity arose.

Took the leap of faith, landed in this beautiful (though slightly burnt) country and landed my first job just over a week later.

Spent 2.5 years with RB who were kind enough to sponsor my visa, ran 2 tenders for contract packing - first introducing a new supplier for the first time in 8 years, second to move the operation to RBs new warehouse, offshored point of sales materials manufacturing and implemented safety stock models massively reducing lead-times, then went on to work for an iconic Australian brand in Blackmores. RB was a great company to work for (would've been even better had I wanted to move back to Europe to grow within their structures).

Blackmores days - 3 CEOs, 3 COOs and 5 line managers later, my passion project which I had been pushing for from day 1 - building a bespoke SRM solution - got approved and I've been the driving and creative force as the Product Owner ever since.

Now, weeks away from my Australian citizenship ceremony, after releasing the solution to the broader business and training users, with the developers resources moving slowly towards other initiatives it is time to consider moving on.

And that's where NBN came along...

PS. If the website doesn't tell it - I'm learning some programming so I can speak the same language (at least to some extent) with IT crowd or do some automation myself.
